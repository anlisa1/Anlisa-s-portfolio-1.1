    Problem: Financial stress (mental stress on money issues) causes college students to have lower performances in college.              

Root Cause 1:
An unclear idea of your financial future causes part of the financial stress as in worries about your money not the other type of financial stress because the unknown causes fear and uncertainty anxiety, combined it adds onto stress.

  • Evidence

    “Compared to students who did not expect to have any student loan debt at graduation, students who expected to have below average debt (OR = 1.686), average debt (OR = 2.023), and above average debt (OR=3.024) were significantly more likely to report financial stress”( page 30).

  • Reasoning

    This generally says that those who have expected graduation debt were more financially stressed than those who didn’t have expected graduation debt. People who have expected graduation debt feel unclear about their financial future because they are expected to eventually pay it off and there are also interest rates on top of it. Not knowing when you can pay it off, or even if you can free yourself of debt at all in this lifetime, distracts people from their studies and further drags them down as their grades drop, and it can potentially turn into a cycle of stress.

  • Implications

    Now that we know that what is expected of them adds pressure because they do not know if they can do it. A solution that this will tilt towards is probably strengthening their goal of why they decided to go to college to begin with. As long as their goals are strengthened and perhaps connect to facets of their career choices to lighten some career burdens, the debt won't disappear but may become a driving force for the students to work harder.

  • Source

    https://www.researchgate.net/publication/271109392_ Factors_Related_to_Financial_Stress_among_College_Students

  • Evidence

    ”Perceived stress is defined as the extent to which an individual perceives that their demands exceed their ability to cope, and is a major factor in academic disruption and poor academic performance”

  • Reasoning

    This says that financial strains are less of an issue than perceived stress as it is not tackled on much. The article also mentioned not in here but that ⅘ top stressors were related to personal finance. Which means to say that perceived stress, and their perceived ability to cope with financial demands is the cause of financial stress. Whether it is the future or present their perception of their own ability to pay back debt or support themselves in the future causes stress.

  • Implications

    What this redirects me to is the importance of perceived stress. How you perceive yourself and how you perceive going to college and debt. Perhaps a method to empower college students and to boost their self confidence that they do have the ability to make money and pay off their debt.

  • Source

    https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5086162/

Root Cause 2:
Your current financial situation may also cause stress about money because you may want to work more to support yourself, or do not have the means or money to support yourself. Also too much stress on money may cloud your mind, an make you unable to focus on your studies.

  • Evidence

    “University students experienced significant levels of stress, anxiety, and depression related to the pandemic; therefore, worsening their mental health conditions” and ”poor indoor quality is the determinant that is most associated with more moderate/severe and severe depressive symptomatology in university students”.

  • Reasoning

    A basic need is good air quality, but without good air quality you have more people stressed they said, in the times of the pandemic. The students did not have the means of supporting themselves with good quality air or were simply too busy to ever focus on their health because they had to deal with financial issues. This associates the living condition, an indicator of your financial situation, to more financial stress being caused as bad quality air can affect your health and emotions.

  • Implications

    The implications are that we should also maybe focus on the college students' personal living conditions. As not being able to live well adds on to the stress.

Since there financial situation right now is not the best they have to make a way to earn money to support themselves, which adds more stress if the person gets barely any time to relax.

    Maybe I can go after perhaps a way to have college students have better hygiene, health and overall living conditions. We need to have them learn about balance.
